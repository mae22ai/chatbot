A Study on the Critical Consideration on Concessive
Endings in Korean Language Education
Seo, Huijeong․Hong, Yunki
This paper aims to clarify the meaning of concessive endings and suggest the
educational methods of them in Korean language education. The meaning of concession
that is presented as a word does not coincide with the meaning of concession that is
presented as a grammatical term. Also, concessive endings such as ‘-아도’, ‘-더라도’, ‘-
ㄹ지라도’, ‘-ㄴ들’ are frequently able to be replaced by contrastive endings like ‘-지
만’, ‘-는데’. That is why this paper raises doubts about the meaning of concession.
A survey of 83 advanced Korean language learners has been conducted in order to
check out how Korean language learners understand the meaning of concession. It was
revealed that Korean language learners marked the meaning of concessive endings down
as contrast, assumption as well as concession. Also, that survey implies that concession can
서희정(제1저자) not be used as a grammatical term, and then there is a possibility that concessive endings
can not be presented with concession, but contrast or assumption as their meaning.
